# The 24-Form Tai Chi Chuan — Structure & Overview

The 24-form is the **standard popular set** of Tai Chi Chuan in mainland China and most of the world. It was compiled in **1956** by the Chinese National Physical Education Committee, based on the traditional Yang-style 108-form, with the goal of **making Tai Chi accessible to ordinary people** during the new nation's reconstruction era.

## Why 24 postures?

- **Easy to memorize:** 24 instead of 108 — beginners can learn the set in 3-6 months of regular practice
- **Deep enough:** Still contains all basic movement groups (beginning, one-side arms, bend/stand, big steps, ending)
- **Widely taught:** Available in most clubs, parks, health centers
- **Scientifically studied:** Most studied set in sports-medicine research (because it is easy to standardize)

## Four major groups

| Group | Postures | Characteristics | Main effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Beginning/Ending** | 1, 24 | Gather intention, return | Nervous system, focus |
| **One-side arms** | 2-7 | Open one-side yang meridians | Arm qi and blood |
| **Bend/Stand** | 8-13 | Release waist, open kidney qi | Kidneys, waist, tendons |
| **Big steps** | 14-23 | Footwork, weight shifting | Legs, hips, balance |

## Comparison with other sets

| Set | Postures | Time | Audience |
|---|---|---|---|
| **24-form (1956)** | 24 | ~6 min | Beginners, health |
| **42-form (competition)** | 42 | ~10 min | Competition, advanced |
| **48-form (CCCP)** | 48 | ~12 min | Traditional |
| **88-form (Yang)** | 88 | ~25 min | Hsing Yi form |
| **108-form Yang** | 108 | ~30 min | Complete |

## The full 24-posture list

1. **Commencing Form** (起勢) — Qǐshì
2. **Parting the Wild Horse's Mane** (左右野馬分鬃) — Zuoyou Yemǎ Fēnzōng
3. **White Crane Spreads Its Wings** (白鶴亮翅) — Báihè Liàngchì
4. **Brush Knee and Twist Step** (左右摟膝拗步) — Zuoyou Lōuxī ǎobù
5. **Strum the Pipa** (手揮琵琶) — Shǒu Huī Pípá
6. **Repulse the Monkey** (左右倒卷肱) — Zuoyou Dǎo Juǎn Gōng
7-8. **Grasp the Sparrow's Tail** (攬雀尾) — Lǎn Quèwěi (left and right)
9. **Single Whip** (單鞭) — Dānbiān
10. **Cloud Hands** (雲手) — Yúnshǒu
11. **High Pat on Horse** (左右高探馬) — Zuoyou Gāo Tànmǎ
12. **Right Heel Kick** (右蹬腳) — Yòu Dēngjiǎo
13. **Strike Opponent's Ears with Both Fists** (雙峰貫耳) — Shuāngfēng Guàněr
14. **Turn Body Left Heel Kick** (左蹬腳) — Zuoyou Dēngjiǎo
15. **Turn Body White Crane Spreads Wings** (轉身白鶴亮翅) — Zhuǎnshēn Báihè Liàngchì
16. **Brush Knee and Twist Step** (左右行步拗步) — Zuoyou Xíngbù ǎobù
17. **Right Heel Kick** (右蹬腳) — Yòu Dēngjiǎo
18. **Strike Opponent's Ears with Both Fists** (雙峰貫耳) — repeat
19-20. **Cross Hands** (左右十字手) — Zuoyou Shízìshǒu
21. **Turn Body Fist Strike** (轉身指襠捶) — Zhuǎnshēn Zhǐdāngchuí
22-23. **Grasp the Sparrow's Tail** (攬雀尾) — closing
24. **Closing Form** (收勢) — Shōushì

## Learning path

| Week | Goal |
|---|---|
| 1-4 | Learn first 4 postures, correct shape |
| 5-8 | Learn postures 5-12, begin connecting smoothly |
| 9-12 | Learn postures 13-20, focus on footwork |
| 13-16 | Learn postures 21-24, can complete one round |
| 17-24 | Complete 6-minute form, focus on breathing |
| 25-52 | Advanced: relax, intention leads qi, control center |
